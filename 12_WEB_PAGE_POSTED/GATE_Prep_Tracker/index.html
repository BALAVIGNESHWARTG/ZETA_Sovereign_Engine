You completely bypass Operating Systems, Computer Architecture, Compilers, and Networks. Highly efficient.

A strong DA score qualifies you directly for the M.Tech and M.S. (by Research) programs at the Wadhwani School.

Master the language of AI. You cannot do ML without Math.

  • Probability: Bayes, Distributions, Expectation.
  • Statistics: CLT, Hypothesis Testing (z/t/chi).
  • Linear Algebra: Matrices, Gaussian elimination, Rank.

Implement conceptually. Understand math behind algorithms.

  • Finish Linear Algebra (SVD, Eigen) & Calculus.
  • Machine Learning: Regression, Logistic, SVM, Trees, PCA, K-Means, MLE, Bias-Variance.
  • Artificial Intelligence: Search (A*), Minimax, Logic.
  • Checkpoint: Whole syllabus covered once. You can explain ML assumptions & objectives.

Dec 2026 - Jan 2027

Phase 3: Systems & Practice Ramp-up

Secure deterministic coding marks and start past papers.

  • DBMS & DSA: Normalization, SQL, Python tracing, Big-O.
  • Start daily General Aptitude sets.
  • Solve official DA PYQs (2024, 2025, 2026) subject by subject.
  • Checkpoint: PYQs solved. Error log started.

Jan 2027 - Feb 2027

Phase 4: Peak & Revise

Build exam temperament and consolidate.

  • Take 10-15 full-length 3-hour mocks.
  • Revise purely from your one-page formula sheets and Error Log.
  • Lock in time-management strategy.

Exam Strategy & Mock Tests

How the paper is actually tested—and beaten. 3 hours, 65 questions, 100 marks. Managing the clock is a skill built in mocks, not improvised on exam day.

The 3-Pass Approach

  1. Pass 1 (~90 min): Go front to back. Bank all easy, fast marks. Flag anything needing heavy thought and move on immediately.
  2. Pass 2 (~60 min): Return to flagged questions worth the time. Attempt medium items and longer numericals.
  3. Pass 3 (~30 min): Attempt remaining NAT/MSQs (no penalty). Make calculated 80%-rule decisions on MCQs.

Negative Marking Discipline

  • NAT & MSQ: No penalty. Attempt all of them, always. Even a reasoned guess is free expected value.
  • MCQ: -1/3 for 1-mark, -2/3 for 2-mark. Mark only if 80%+ confident or if you can definitively eliminate 2 options.
  • Never Tunnel: No single question is worth losing 5 others. Stuck for 4+ mins? Flag and leave.

The Mock-Test System (Jan-Feb)

Mocks train speed and temperament. Do not just "take" mocks; analyze them.

  • ✔ Subject Tests first (Dec): Confirm a subject is solid before moving on.
  • ✔ Full 3-Hour Mocks (Jan): Start with 1-2 a week, build to 3. Sit them at the exact time of day as your real GATE slot.
  • ✔ Analyze more than you attempt: Classify every wrong answer as: Concept Gap, Silly Error, or Time Pressure. Fix the root cause.
  • ✔ Track the trend: One low score means nothing; a rising trend across 10-15 mocks is the real signal.

Revision Protocol & Final 30 Days

Most forgotten material was never truly learned. Revision is built on retrieval, not passive re-reading. Close the notes and try to reproduce the formula from memory.

The 1-Page Formula Sheets

Build one page per subject. Make it yourself—the act of building it is half the learning. In the final week, revise ONLY from these 7 pages.

Prob & Stats All 5 distributions (PMF, mean, var), Bayes, CLT, the 3 test statistics.
Machine Learning 1 line per algo (assumption/objective/use), metric formulas, bias-variance.
Linear Algebra Eigenvalue shortcuts (trace/det), rank-nullity, SVD form, projection matrix.
Programming/DSA Big-O table, Python idioms, BFS/DFS traversal templates.

The Last 30 Days (Tapering Phase)

Days 30 - 21

Heavy Mocks & Repair

Full mocks (2-3/week) + same day analysis. Revise the weakest two subjects explicitly based on your error log.

Days 20 - 11

Speed & Polish

Finish any remaining official PYQs. Daily GA practice. Tighten time strategy and virtual calculator speed.

Days 10 - 4

Pure Consolidation

Taper mocks to 1-2. Revise purely from formula sheets and error log. No new topics. Fix logistics (ID, admit card).

Days 3 - 1

Calm & Rest

Light revision only. Normal sleep schedule. Exam day rehearsal. Cramming now hurts memory consolidation.

Mindset, Mistakes & FAQs

The human side of an 8-month climb. Systems are what carry you when motivation inevitably dips.

⚠️ Mistakes That Cost Ranks

  • Learning ML before Math: ML becomes blind memorization without Probability and LinAlg.
  • Passive Study: Watching lectures without solving problems. You must prove it.
  • Skipping Revision: July's foundation is worthless in Dec if never revisited.
  • Ignoring GA: Leaving 15 easy marks on the table.
  • Hoarding Resources: Collecting 10 courses, finishing none. Pick ONE primary source.
